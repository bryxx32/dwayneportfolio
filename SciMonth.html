Science Month

What is the most important thing I learned from the event?

I learned a lot about science—the terms, definitions, and how to cooperate with my teammates in creating a research plan from scratch within a limited time. I learned how to work under pressure and make the most of every opportunity given to me.

How can I apply what I have learned in real-life situations?

I can apply this in real life through the knowledge I gained about science and research. I now understand how things work and why they happen. Creating a research plan under time pressure taught me to think faster, generate innovative ideas, and improve based on feedback from panelists.

Did I actively participate in the event? How?

Yes, I participated in this event as the representative of my section in both the Stemazing competition and the Science Quiz Bee.

If I were to teach this topic or subject to a classmate, how would I explain it?

I would explain what happens during Science Month, why it’s important, how to create a research plan, and what it feels like to participate in scientific events.

Why is it important to have an event (per subject)? Explain your answer.

It is important to have an event in Science because it helps us understand how the world works. Science explains why things happen and sparks curiosity. It is especially important in a Science High School where innovation and discovery are valued.
